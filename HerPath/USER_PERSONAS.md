# User Personas

## Persona 1: Sarah - The High School Student

**Demographics:**

- Age: 17
- Location: Seattle, Washington
- Education: High school junior
- Income: Part-time job ($200-300/month)
- Tech Savviness: High (uses smartphone daily, comfortable with apps)

**Goals:**

- Learn basic financial skills before college
- Understand how to manage her part-time job earnings
- Prepare for financial independence
- Build good money habits early

**Pain Points:**

- Limited financial knowledge from school or family
- Feels overwhelmed by financial terminology
- Wants to save for college but doesn't know where to start
- Worried about making financial mistakes

**User Journey:**
Sarah discovers HerPath through a school counselor recommendation. She creates an account and starts with Section 1: Financial Health. She appreciates the bite-sized modules and video content that makes complex topics accessible. She tracks her progress and feels motivated by the visual progress bar. By completing modules on budgeting and saving, she creates her first budget and opens a savings account.

**Key Needs:**

- Simple, easy-to-understand explanations
- Visual learning through videos
- Practical action items she can implement immediately
- Progress tracking to stay motivated
- Mobile-friendly interface for learning on-the-go

## Persona 2: Maria - The College Freshman

**Demographics:**

- Age: 19
- Location: University of Washington campus
- Education: First-year college student
- Income: Student loans, part-time work-study ($400/month)
- Tech Savviness: Very High (uses multiple apps daily)

**Goals:**

- Manage student loan money responsibly
- Learn about credit and building credit history
- Understand taxes for part-time work
- Avoid common financial pitfalls in college

**Pain Points:**

- Receiving large loan disbursements and unsure how to budget
- Getting credit card offers but doesn't understand credit
- Needs to file taxes for the first time
- Wants to build credit but doesn't know how

**User Journey:**
Maria finds HerPath while researching financial aid information. She's particularly interested in the Credit Basics and Federal Financial Aid sections. She uses the platform to learn about credit scores, credit cards, and how to build credit safely. She also completes the Tax modules to prepare for tax season. The progress tracking helps her stay organized and ensures she doesn't miss important topics.

**Key Needs:**

- Comprehensive information on credit and loans
- Step-by-step guidance for first-time financial tasks
- Ability to learn at her own pace around class schedule
- Reliable, trustworthy information source
- Progress tracking to ensure she covers all topics

## Persona 3: Jessica - The Recent Graduate

**Demographics:**

- Age: 23
- Location: Seattle, Washington
- Education: Recent college graduate
- Income: Entry-level job ($3,500/month)
- Tech Savviness: High (comfortable with web and mobile apps)

**Goals:**

- Manage first full-time salary effectively
- Build emergency fund
- Start investing for the future
- Understand retirement planning basics
- Pay off student loans strategically

**Pain Points:**

- Overwhelmed by "adult" financial responsibilities
- Receives first real paycheck and doesn't understand deductions
- Wants to invest but doesn't know where to start
- Feels behind peers who had financial education earlier
- Needs to make important financial decisions quickly

**User Journey:**
Jessica graduates and lands her first job. She realizes she lacks financial knowledge and searches for resources. She finds HerPath and appreciates that it's designed for young women. She starts with Income Basics to understand her paycheck, then moves through Budgeting and Smart Spending modules. She uses the Savings section to build her emergency fund and eventually explores the Investing section. The platform helps her feel more confident making financial decisions.

**Key Needs:**

- Comprehensive curriculum covering all financial topics
- Professional, trustworthy content
- Practical advice for real-world situations
- Progress tracking to ensure she covers everything
- Ability to reference content later when needed

## Persona 4: Emma - The Self-Learner

**Demographics:**

- Age: 20
- Location: Remote/Online
- Education: Some college, taking gap year
- Income: Freelance work ($800-1200/month, variable)
- Tech Savviness: Very High

**Goals:**

- Learn financial literacy independently
- Understand freelancer-specific financial topics (taxes, irregular income)
- Build financial confidence
- Create sustainable financial habits

**Pain Points:**

- Irregular income makes budgeting challenging
- Needs to understand self-employment taxes
- Wants structured learning but flexible schedule
- Prefers self-paced education over formal classes

**User Journey:**
Emma is taking a gap year and working freelance. She wants to use this time to improve her financial literacy. She discovers HerPath and appreciates that it's free and self-paced. She works through modules systematically, focusing on Budgeting (for irregular income), Taxes (for self-employment), and Smart Spending. She uses the progress tracking to ensure she completes all sections. The platform becomes her primary financial education resource.

**Key Needs:**

- Self-paced learning structure
- Comprehensive content she can reference
- Mobile access for learning anywhere
- Progress tracking to stay organized
- Free access (important as a freelancer with variable income)

---

## Common Characteristics Across Personas

All personas share:

- Desire for financial independence and confidence
- Preference for visual and interactive learning
- Need for trustworthy, reliable information
- Appreciation for free, accessible education
- Interest in progress tracking and gamification elements
- Mobile-first usage patterns
- Need for practical, actionable advice

## Design Implications

Based on these personas, HerPath is designed to:

- Provide clear, accessible explanations suitable for beginners
- Offer visual learning through embedded videos
- Include practical action items for immediate application
- Support mobile and desktop usage
- Track progress to maintain motivation
- Present content in digestible, module-based format
- Create a supportive, non-intimidating learning environment
